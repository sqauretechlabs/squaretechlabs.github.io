Cloud Migration

Migration

  • Ashish Gupta
  • Jan 1, 2021

Migrating to the cloud has become a key enabler for organizations as they drive change programs and transformation initiatives. Cloud hosting helps achieve scale, flexibility, and robust software delivery practices such as test-driven development, working in iterations, infrastructure as code, continuous integration and continuous delivery. These “ways of working” raise the quality bar for delivery teams by establishing practices that incorporate resilience and quality into the development lifecycle.

Leveraging Cevo’s skills and knowledge in cloud migration, our customers realize an array of ongoing benefits, from compelling events around data centre equipment renewals, cost optimization and fully utilized infrastructure, through to improved security and data governance, greater business agility with more productive and mobile teams, and better positioning for attracting and retaining top talent.

Our extensive experience in designing, building, and operating cloud-native architectures and assisting with the migration and re-architecture of existing on-prem systems has helped numerous customers avoid costly mistakes and realize the true value.

WORKLOAD MIGRATION

With experience in migrating a range of workloads including some of the most complex, our service covers advice and planning through to architecture, delivery assistance and operational guidance.

RE-ARCHITECTURE AND RE-PLATFORMING

We apply our extensive experience in designing, building and operating cloud-native architectures to create long-term vision and strategic returns on investment for our customers. Benefits range from talent attraction and retention, business agility and modern architecture with minimal operational overhead

TOTAL COST OF OWNERSHIP

We assist customers by providing visibility to identify which activities are best positioned for migration. A total cost of ownership (TCO) analysis looks at the advantages of a cloud environment as well as the real cost of running on-prem infrastructure vs cloud.
